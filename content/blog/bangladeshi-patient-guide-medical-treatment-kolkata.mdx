Every year, thousands of Bangladeshi patients travel to Kolkata for medical treatment — drawn by advanced hospitals, experienced specialists, and costs that remain far more accessible than Western alternatives. But navigating a foreign healthcare system while managing an illness can be overwhelming without the right guidance. Here's what you need to know before you travel.

## Step 1: Medical visa requirements

Bangladeshi patients need a **Medical Visa**, not a standard tourist visa, to seek treatment in India. This typically requires an invitation letter from the treating Indian hospital, your medical records showing the condition, and passport-sized photos and standard visa documentation. Processing usually takes a few days to a couple of weeks, so planning ahead matters, especially for non-emergency treatments.

## Step 2: Choosing the right hospital

Kolkata is home to several highly regarded hospitals for cardiac care, oncology, orthopedics, neurology, and organ transplants. The right choice depends on your specific condition, the specialist's experience with similar cases, and — importantly — how well the hospital supports international patients with language assistance and coordination.

## Step 3: Getting a second opinion before you travel

Reputable medical tourism facilitators can arrange a remote consultation or review of your reports by a Kolkata-based specialist before you even book travel — helping confirm the treatment plan and cost estimate in advance, so there are no surprises on arrival.

## Step 4: Accommodation and stay

Depending on treatment duration, patients and accompanying family typically need short-term serviced accommodation near the hospital, longer-term stays for extended treatment or recovery, and support with local transport between accommodation, hospital, and diagnostics.

## Step 5: Language and cultural comfort

While many doctors and hospital staff in Kolkata communicate comfortably in Bengali, having a dedicated coordinator who understands both the healthcare system and the needs of Bangladeshi patients makes an enormous difference in reducing stress during treatment.

<Callout type="tip" title="Step 6: Managing costs transparently">
  Treatment costs in Kolkata are typically a fraction of equivalent care in Western countries, but costs can still vary significantly by hospital and procedure. Ask for a detailed, written cost estimate covering consultation, procedure, hospital stay, and any post-treatment follow-up before committing.
</Callout>
