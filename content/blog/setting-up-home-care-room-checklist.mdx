When a loved one needs ongoing care at home — whether after surgery, during illness, or for long-term senior care — getting the room set up properly makes a bigger difference than most families expect. A well-prepared space reduces risk, eases caregiving, and speeds recovery. Here's the checklist we walk families through at Suraksha24.

## 1. The right bed

An adjustable hospital bed (manual or electric) allows the patient to be positioned upright for meals or breathing comfort, and flat for sleep — reducing strain on caregivers who would otherwise need to physically adjust the patient.

## 2. Pressure-relief mattress

For patients with limited mobility, a standard mattress increases the risk of bedsores within days. An air or foam pressure-relief mattress is essential, not optional, for anyone bedbound for extended periods.

## 3. Mobility aids near the bed

A walker, cane, or wheelchair positioned within easy reach reduces fall risk during transfers — many falls happen precisely because mobility aids are "just a few steps away" instead of right there.

## 4. Bathroom safety modifications

Grab bars, a raised toilet seat, and a shower chair dramatically reduce fall risk in the highest-risk room of the house. This is one of the most overlooked but important investments.

## 5. Monitoring equipment, if medically needed

For patients with cardiac, respiratory, or post-surgical conditions, a pulse oximeter or basic vitals monitor at home allows early detection of complications before they become emergencies.

## 6. Good lighting and clear pathways

Simple but critical: remove loose rugs, ensure night lighting near the bed and bathroom path, and keep walkways completely clear of clutter or cords.

## 7. Emergency contact list, visibly posted

Doctor's number, nearest hospital, ambulance service, and your home care provider's contact — posted where any caregiver or family member can find it instantly during a crisis.

<Callout type="tip" title="Comfort elements that aid recovery">
  Don't underestimate the role of natural light, a comfortable chair for visitors, and personal items nearby — emotional comfort genuinely supports physical healing.
</Callout>
