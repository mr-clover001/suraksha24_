Falls are one of the leading causes of serious injury among seniors — but the good news is that a large percentage of falls are preventable through simple, consistent strength and balance exercises. At Suraksha24, our physiotherapists build fall-prevention routines into almost every senior care plan. Here are five exercises that make a real difference.

## 1. Sit-to-stand

From a sturdy chair, practice standing up without using hands, then sitting back down slowly. This builds leg strength critical for daily balance and reduces the most common cause of falls — weak legs during transfers.

*Start with 5–8 repetitions, once or twice a day, always with supervision initially.*

## 2. Heel-to-toe walk

Walking in a straight line, placing the heel of one foot directly in front of the toes of the other, improves balance and coordination — much like a sobriety test, but for strengthening stability.

*Practice near a wall or counter for support.*

## 3. Standing marches

Holding onto a chair or counter, lift one knee at a time as if marching in place. This strengthens hip flexors, which are essential for safe walking and stair use.

## 4. Ankle circles and toe raises

Weak ankles are an overlooked fall risk. Simple ankle circles and rising onto toes (while holding support) improve stability on uneven surfaces.

## 5. Wall push-ups

Standing arm's length from a wall, gently push in and out. This builds upper body strength, which helps in recovering balance if a stumble occurs.

<Callout type="info" title="A word of caution">
  These exercises are safe for most seniors, but anyone with recent surgery, severe osteoporosis, or cardiac conditions should only start under professional physiotherapy guidance.
</Callout>
