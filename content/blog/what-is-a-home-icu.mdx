For many families, the phrase "home ICU" sounds almost contradictory — isn't intensive care something that can only happen in a hospital? The truth is, modern home healthcare has made it possible to deliver critical, hospital-grade care in a patient's own bedroom, safely and effectively, for the right cases.

## What a home ICU setup actually includes

A home ICU typically involves:

- ICU-grade hospital bed with pressure-relief mattress
- Multi-para monitor tracking heart rate, oxygen saturation, blood pressure, and ECG
- Oxygen concentrator or cylinder support, and in some cases ventilator support
- Infusion pumps for precise medication delivery
- Trained critical care nurses, often on 24-hour rotation
- Regular physician oversight, either in-person or via tele-consultation

## Who is a candidate for home ICU care?

Home ICU is typically suitable for patients who are medically stable enough to be moved but still require continuous monitoring — for example, patients recovering from a major cardiac event, those on long-term ventilator support, advanced-stage illness requiring comfort-focused critical care, or post-ICU discharge patients who still need close observation.

It is not a replacement for hospital ICU in acute, unstable emergencies — but for stabilized patients, it can be dramatically more comfortable and, in many cases, safer from a hospital-acquired infection standpoint.

## Why families choose home ICU

Beyond cost savings compared to prolonged hospital stays, the emotional and psychological benefit is significant. Patients often respond better in familiar surroundings, and families can stay closely involved in care decisions and daily presence — something hospital ICU visiting hours rarely allow.

<Callout type="info" title="The non-negotiables for safety">
  A home ICU is only as safe as the team running it. Look for 24/7 nursing coverage (not just periodic visits), a clear emergency escalation protocol to the nearest hospital, and regular physician review — not just equipment placed in a room.
</Callout>
