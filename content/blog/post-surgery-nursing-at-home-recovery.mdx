Surgery is often the "easy" part. What happens in the weeks after — the dressing changes, the pain management, the fear of complications — is where most families feel unprepared. At Suraksha24, we've supported hundreds of post-operative recoveries at home across Kolkata, and here's what that journey typically looks like.

## Week 1: Stabilization

Immediately after discharge, the focus is on wound care, infection prevention, and pain control. A skilled nurse monitors vitals, changes dressings under sterile technique, and watches for early warning signs like fever or unusual swelling — issues families often miss until they escalate.

## Week 2–3: Mobility begins

This is where physiotherapy typically joins the care plan. Gentle movement prevents complications like blood clots and muscle stiffness while protecting the surgical site. Nurses coordinate closely with physiotherapists so recovery isn't rushed or delayed.

## Week 3–6: Independence rebuilding

As healing progresses, care shifts from "doing for" the patient to "helping the patient do for themselves" — bathing, walking, and daily activities with supervision rather than full assistance.

## Common mistakes families make

- Stopping professional care too early because the patient "looks fine"
- Missing subtle infection signs (low-grade fever, redness spreading)
- Under-managing pain, which slows mobility and recovery
- Not following the surgeon's follow-up schedule strictly

<Callout type="info" title="Why home recovery often works better">
  Hospitals carry higher infection risk for recovering patients, and the unfamiliar environment can increase anxiety, especially in elderly patients. Recovering at home, with skilled nursing support, often leads to faster, calmer healing — provided the care is genuinely professional.
</Callout>
